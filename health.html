Symptoms of Pneumonia


Signs and symptoms of pneumonia may include:

  • Chest pain when you breathe or cough
  • Confusion or changes in mental awareness (in adults age 65 and older)
  • Cough, which may produce phlegm
  • Fatigue
  • Fever, sweating and shaking chills
  • Lower than normal body temperature (in adults older than age 65 and people with weak immune systems)
  • Nausea, vomiting or diarrhea
  • Shortness of breath


Types of pneumonia:

  • Bacterial pneumonia: caused by bacteria and is the most common type
  • Viral pneumonia: caused by viruses, including the flu virus and respiratory syncytial virus (RSV)
  • Fungal pneumonia: caused by fungi, such as Pneumocystis jirovecii
  • Aspiration pneumonia: occurs when you inhale food, drink, or saliva into your lungs
  • Atypical pneumonia: caused by atypical bacteria, such as Mycoplasma pneumoniae or Legionella pneumophila


Precautions for pneumonia:

  • Wash your hands frequently with soap and water, or use hand sanitizer containing at least 60% alcohol
  • Avoid close contact with people who have respiratory infections
  • Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when coughing or sneezing
  • Avoid touching your face, especially your eyes, nose, and mouth
  • Maintain good respiratory hygiene by coughing into a tissue or your elbow, and disposing of used tissues properly
  • Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations, including the pneumonia vaccine
  • Practice good overall health habits, including getting enough sleep, eating a healthy diet, and staying physically active
  • Avoid smoking and exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Manage chronic conditions, such as diabetes or heart disease, as directed by your healthcare provider


More Information

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, causing cough, difficulty breathing, chest pain, and fever. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.
